Policy Change: Neutral - The privacy policy clearly states that it may be amended from time to time to reflect changes and additions. While this indicates transparency and a commitment to updating the policy, the neutral sentiment is chosen as it depends on how effectively users are informed about these changes and whether they have the opportunity to review the updated policy before relying on its provisions.
First Party Collection/Use: Neutral - The privacy policy outlines Valve's collection and use of personally identifiable information from users, stating that information is collected when users create a Steam account or engage with Valve's products and services. Valve uses this information internally to deliver, develop, and improve its offerings, as well as for communication purposes with users who opt-in. While Valve states that personally identifiable information is collected on a voluntary basis, it also mentions that for certain products and services, providing such information may be a requirement. The policy also addresses the sharing of information with third parties under specific circumstances. Overall, the discussion on first-party collection and use appears to be neutral, providing transparency about the practices without explicitly leaning towards being overly user-friendly or user-risky.
Data Retention: Neutral - The privacy policy does not explicitly mention a specific data retention period or practice. While it states that personally identifiable information will be collected, processed, and stored, it does not provide detailed information on how long this data will be retained. This lack of clarity may lead to uncertainty for users regarding the retention of their information, resulting in a neutral sentiment classification.
International and Specific Audiences: Neutral - The privacy policy discusses the collection, processing, and storage of personally identifiable information provided by users in the EU and mentions that Valve Corporation complies with the Safe Harbor framework developed by the U.S. Department of Commerce and the European Union. While the policy addresses the handling of information from international users, it does not explicitly outline specific measures or considerations for users from specific regions or countries, which could be seen as a user-risky practice. Overall, the sentiment for this category is neutral as the policy provides some information but lacks specific details for international and specific audiences.
Third Party Sharing/Collection: Neutral - The privacy policy discusses third-party sharing and collection practices in various sections. Valve mentions that external websites and companies with links to and from Valve's online sites may collect personally identifiable information about users, and third-party publishers may also collect such information. Valve clarifies that its privacy policy does not extend to these external entities and advises users to refer directly to these companies and websites regarding their privacy policies. While Valve states that it may allow third parties to access and use personally identifiable information for specific services under contract, it also emphasizes that it will not share personally identifiable information with third parties for marketing purposes without user consent. Overall, the policy provides transparency about third-party involvement but also highlights the importance of users being aware of and reviewing the privacy practices of external entities, leading to a neutral sentiment classification.
User Choice/Control: Positive - The privacy policy provides users with clear options and control over their personally identifiable information. Users are given the choice to opt out of promotional email communications, and they can also request corrections, updates, or removal of their personally identifiable information. The policy emphasizes user control by allowing users to manage their preferences and contact Valve for any privacy-related concerns.
User Access, Edit and Deletion: Positive - The privacy policy provides clear instructions for users to access, edit, and delete their personally identifiable information maintained by Valve. It states that users can contact Valve if they wish to review, access, amend, correct, or remove their information. The policy also mentions that users have the option to opt out of promotional email communications from Valve. Overall, the policy demonstrates a user-friendly practice by offering users control over their personal data.
